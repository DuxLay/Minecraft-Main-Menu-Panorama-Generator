### 🌌 DuxLay 360 Splitter
The Lightweight Tool for Minecraft Main Menu Screen Panoramas

### 🚀 What is DuxLay 360 Splitter?

DuxLay 360 Splitter is a clean, modern, and efficient utility designed to take any 360° panoramic image (equirectangular projection) and instantly slice it into the six required cubic faces (panorama_0 through panorama_5) needed for Minecraft custom resource packs.

It is designed for creators who want to transform their game’s title screen into a truly immersive experience without the headache of manual cropping or complex image manipulation software.

### ✨ Key Features
Instant Conversion: Just load your 360° image, and the tool will automatically handle the slicing, orientation, and perfect grid layout required by Minecraft.

Modern UI: A clean, user-friendly interface that includes an automatic Dark Mode, ensuring your eyes stay comfortable while you work.

Multilingual Support: Seamlessly switch between Spanish and English to suit your workflow.

Zero Dependencies Required: Since this is a portable tool, you don't need to install complex libraries or heavy compilers; just run the .exe and start creating.

Lightweight: Designed to be extremely efficient, even on low-end systems.

### 🛡️ Windows SmartScreen / Anti-Virus Notice
Since this is an open-source tool and does not carry a costly commercial digital certificate, Windows SmartScreen might display an "Unknown Publisher" or "Unsafe file" warning when launching the .exe for the first time.

This is a false positive. To run the app, simply click on "More Info" and then "Run Anyway". Since the complete Python source code (DuxLay360Splitter.py) is fully exposed right here in this repository, anyone can inspect every single line of code to verify its absolute security and privacy.

### 📖 The Story Behind the Tool
I wanted a custom 360° panorama for my Minecraft Title Screen. Simple enough, right?

Wrong. Every tutorial I found involved Photoshop, a bunch of manual screenshots, stitching everything together by hand... it was a whole ordeal. And the few tools that existed online were either broken, abandoned, or just didn't do what I needed. I literally searched for hours and found **nothing**. Which honestly surprised me, like, how does this not exist yet?

So I did the only reasonable thing: I built it myself

Here's the thing though **I'm not a developer**. Like, at all. I'm just a regular dude who wanted a cool loading screen for Minecraft and ended up coding at 4am trying to make it work. This is my **first ever piece of software**. I don't have a CS degree, I don't have years of experience, I'm not even studying anything close to programming. I just had an idea and refused to let it go

I built this with the help of AI mainly **Claude** and **Gemini** learning on the fly, debugging stuff I didn't fully understand, and slowly turning a chaotic mess of code into something that actually works. And honestly? I'm really proud of it

I'm open-sourcing it because throughout my life I've used so many free tools built by people I'll never meet, tools that just *existed* on the internet ready to solve my exact problem This is my way of giving that back. If even one person finds this and saves themselves two hours of Photoshop pain, then it was worth it
Now you don't have to suffer through those tutorials. You're welcome 😄
